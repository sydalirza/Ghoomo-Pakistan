Sindh

Sindh is one of the four provinces of Pakistan. It is located in the south-east of the country. Balochistan to its west, Punjab to the north, and the Arabian Sea to the south, the landscape mostly consists of alluvial plains surrounding the Indus River. Sindh is the third-largest province by area and second-largest by population.The majority of the residents speak the Sindhi language. Also, there are many places to discover in this province.

Mohenjodaro

Mohenjodaro also known as “Mound of the dead men” is one of the largest and best-preserved city of the Indus civilization. Located at the west of the Indus River in Larkana District, this ancient city was built around 2500 BC about the same time the great pyramids were being built in Egypt. The city is divided into two districts the Citadel and the LowerTown the Citadel is home to the city's exceptional monuments including the great baths (a 900 square foot tank fed from the Indus River). Mohenjodaro also had a sophisticated water system. Houses had baths and toilets and the town featured both an elaborate sewage system and fresh water and 700 wells throughout the city.

Keenjhar Lake

Keenjhar Lake, well-known as Kalri Lake, is one of the most wildly underrated tourist spot in Sindh.It as approxiamtely 37 kms far from thatta city.Covering an area of 134 km sq,makes it the second-largest freshwater lake in Pakistan. The lake is believed to have been formed by the merging of two lakes, Sonehri and Keenjhar. It has been declared as a ramsar site and wildlife sanctuary. Not only does it provides a favorable habitat of several birds, the scenic beauty of the lake makes it a perfect spot for peace and nature.

Kot Diji Fort

Kotdiji fort is located in the Khairpur district, Sindh. Kot Diji Fort, meaning "Fort of the Daughter" and formally known as Fort Ahmadabad, dominates the town of Kot Diji in the Khairpur District, Pakistan about 25 miles east of the Indus River. The fort was built between 1785 to 1795 by Mir Sohrab Khan Talpur, founder of the Kingdom of Upper Sindh in 1783. In addition to the fort, a 5 kilometer, 12 feet wide defensive mud wall was built around the city. This wall had bastions throughout its length and a huge iron gate served as the city's only entrance. Due to its geographical location,the civilants enjoyed advantage over enemies as they were became exauseted and searched for irrgation lands for water

Frere Hall

Frere Hall is a construction in Karachi for those people who are fond of music, art and history. The style of the building is Venetian-Gotic Style that also mingles elements of British architecture with local architectural elements. The building features multiple pointed arches, ribbed vaults, quatrefoils, and flying buttresses. Carving on the walls and beautifully articulated mosaic designs are visible on multiple walls and pillars. A tall octagonal tower is located in one of the building's corner that is crowned by an iron cage. The roof of the hall is coated with Munzal Metal.

Mazar-e-Quaid

Mazar-e-Quaid ( Jinnah Mausoleum or the National Mausoleum), is the last resting place of Quaid-e-Azam, the founder of Pakistan. Designed in a 1960s modernist style, it was completed in 1971, and is an iconic symbol of Karachi as well as one of the most popular tourist sites in the city. The mausoleum complex also contains the tomb of Jinnah's sister, Māder-e Millat ("Mother of the Nation") Fatimah Jinnah, as well as those of Liaquat Ali Khan and Nurul Amin, the first and eight of Pakistan respectively.
