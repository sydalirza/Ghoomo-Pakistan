Gilgit-Baltistan

Covering an area of 72,971 km2 and mostly those which are mountainous. Gilgit-Baltistan is a non-provisionally province, disputed territory also known as “the Northern Areas”. It is administered by Pakistan and all of the residents of the area are Muslims. As estimated population report reveals that more than 1.8 million people live there. Due to its location, it proves to be the most important tourist spot. People from abroad mostly visit this place because of its scenic beauty.

Hunza Valley

One of the main tourist spot of the country due to its location,is the well-known Hunza Valley.A land surrounded by beautiful rugged and snow-capped mountains and only at a distance of 100 Kms. from Gilgit, Hunza is a small town on Karakorum Highway.It is located at an altitude of 7000 – 800 feet. The reasons behind its scenic beauty are beautiful lakes, high mountains, and white glaciers. The view of Hunza valley in autumn season is very nice and in winter the sheet of white snow covers the whole valley.

Khunjerab Pass

It is the highest international border-pass to connect Pakistan and China.At an altitude of 4693meters.One interesting thing is ther lies the highest ATM machine in Khunjerab with no other facilities like hotels etc.In winter season it remains closed due to large snowfall.This is mainly constructed in the year of 1982, for trade purpose between the two countries.

Baltit Fort

This fort is situated in karimabad hunza.It is the sign of feudal reigme in Hunza 700 years ago.You will be amazed to see and know about the historic culture of the people of Hunza.Also similar fort known as Altit fort is located 15 kms away in the small town of Altit.

Passu Glacier

Passu Glacier is situated to the south of Passu, a small village in Gojal Valley. This less densly populated area is also known as the home of Glaciers. It is linked with Batura Glacier and many other glaciers in these regions. The glacier is visible on the way towards Khunjerab pass. If you want to enjoy an upper view of the glacier, you can visit Borith lake zero point. After Ghulk in the village. It is an excellent scenery and also a good place to take some rest while heading towards the end line of your journey.

Hispar Glacier

Hispar Glacier is a 49 km long glacier in the Karakoram Mountains of Gilgit–Baltistan, Pakistan which meets the 63 km long Biafo Glacier at the Hispar La at an altitude of 5,128 m to create the world's longest glacial system outside of the polar regions.
